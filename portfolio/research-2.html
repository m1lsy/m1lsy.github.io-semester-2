Reasearch Process

To begin the media campaign research process for Nasmak, I utilized a combination of online research and audience analysis. I focused on understanding the music consumption habits and behaviors of Nasmak’s target audience, specifically younger music fans aged 18-30. This involved researching popular social media platforms, music discovery trends, and concert attendance patterns, while also analyzing the band’s current audience demographics and preferences. The insights gathered helped shape the campaign’s direction and content strategy.

Media Consumption & Engagement

Effective Platforms for Reaching Younger Music Fans:

TikTok is the most effective platform for reaching Gen Z and younger Millennials, with its viral content, trending music challenges, and ease of discovery. Music snippets, short live clips, and fan-generated content are particularly engaging.


Instagram remains popular among both Millennials and Gen Z, combining visual and video content. Reels, behind-the-scenes footage, and concert updates engage fans directly. Features like Stories allow for real-time interaction through polls and Q&As.


YouTube continues to be a go-to platform for discovering music, especially for longer videos like full-length music videos, live recordings, and interviews. Collaborations with YouTubers can expand reach.

MULTI-FORM ENGAGEMENT: Nasmak can maximize audience engagement by producing content that works across these three platforms. Short-form videos (TikTok), Reels (Instagram), and full-length performances (YouTube) will help capture attention from a wide range of music fans.

Audience Demographics & Behavior

Nasmak’s Current Audience:

Demographics: Nasmak’s current audience is predominantly older, ranging from 40-60 years old, as indicated by their Instagram followers and Spotify listener stats. Their fanbase has likely followed the band from their earlier punk years.


Gender: The audience is mixed but leans towards a male-dominated fanbase, typical of the punk genre.


Location: The majority of Nasmak’s fans are based in the Netherlands, with potential clusters in other European cities with strong punk scenes.

Music Preferences & Behavior

Musical Preferences: Nasmak’s fans gravitate towards punk, post-punk, garage rock, and alternative genres, with some likely enjoying indie and experimental music as well.


Concert Attendance: Older fans may attend fewer concerts due to lifestyle factors but still prioritize intimate venues and nostalgia-driven events.


Music Discovery: Older fans typically discover music through traditional means like word of mouth, radio, and blogs, while younger fans use social media (especially TikTok, Instagram) and streaming services (Spotify, YouTube) to discover new music.

Music Discovery Habits of Younger Audiences:

TikTok plays a crucial role in music discovery among younger listeners, with viral challenges and songs gaining significant traction.


Instagram is also a popular discovery tool, with Reels, Stories, and influencer collaborations promoting new music.


Streaming Services: Spotify remains the primary music discovery platform for younger fans, thanks to curated playlists and algorithm-driven recommendations. YouTube is also widely used for live performance videos, lyric videos, and music recommendations.

The Next Step

Recognizing that growing their fanbase was a key challenge, I began brainstorming new strategies to help Nasmak expand their audience. An idea I had was to partner with larger Instagram accounts, such as @eindhovencity, to increase visibility. After reaching out, I discovered that advertising with them was free, and they provided clear instructions for submitting events. This offered a cost-effective way to promote the band. Additionally, I recommended placing posters in the Strijp S area of Eindhoven, a lively and alternative hotspot, to further boost local visibility in places where potential fans would be.

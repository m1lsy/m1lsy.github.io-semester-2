PROJECT ONE/

Branding

CLIENT/

Fontys Health Innovation Minor

PROJECT OVERVIEW

I rebranded this minor study, creating a fresh, modern identity to enhance student engagement and highlight its unique value in healthcare innovation.

SKILLS USED

  • Figma
  • Illustrator
  • Chat GPT
  • Trello
  • Research

DATE

9 September - 3 October 2024

Research Process

“I conducted interviews with students in the target audience to understand key factors influencing their choice of minor. These insights guided the content strategy and brand direction. Next, I identified core brand values and tested colour palettes through a survey, selecting those that best aligned with the desired brand identity."

Design Process

"After completing research, I developed stylescapes and logo concepts. I created a lightbulb logo symbolising healthcare and innovation, iterating based on client feedback. Through presentations, we refined our colour palette and finalised the logo. With the style direction confirmed, I created a full brand guide, detailing the chosen elements."

“The schematic lamp with care cross in the middle is a nice, fresh new variation on the lamp we have. I like it a lot.”

-Client

Reflection

“Reflecting on my first project of the semester, I’m genuinely pleased with the outcome and the entire creative journey. Rebranding a company from start to finish was an exciting process, especially diving into the design aspects. This project allowed me to significantly improve my skills in Adobe Illustrator, particularly in logo creation, where I started with only a basic understanding of the software. Additionally, creating a brand guide for the first time has boosted my confidence, and I now feel much more prepared to tackle similar projects in the future.

Through this experience, I also gained a deeper appreciation for client communication and the importance of gathering feedback before finalizing design decisions. Understanding the value of user research was another crucial takeaway, helping me make design choices that resonate with the intended audience. Overall, this project was not only an enjoyable learning experience but also a pivotal step forward in my development as a designer.”

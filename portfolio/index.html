PROJECT TWO/

Media Campaign

CLIENT/

Nasmak PM

PROJECT OVERVIEW

I built a Media Campaign for Nasmak PM, an experimental electronic band, to help them attract younger audiences, grow their fanbase and have a stronger social media presence.

SKILLS USED

  • Photoshop
  • Trello
  • Research
  • Chat GPT
  • User Testing

DATE

9 September -
3 October 2024

Research Process

I initiated the research phase by developing research questions focused on understanding the band's audience and their preferences. From there, I conducted library research, analyzing articles and relevant sources to gather insights into audience demographics and interests. These findings shaped our media campaign strategy and guided creative decisions around content ideas.

Design Process

To kick off the design process, I suggested that my teammates and I listen to Nasmak PM's music—a unique, improvised experimental electronic band—while creating mood boards. This approach allowed us to visually interpret how each of us connected with their distinctive sound, capturing the diverse emotional responses and energy we felt. These mood boards not only provided valuable insights into the band's avant-garde identity but also served as a key source of inspiration for our design work moving forward. Building on this, I used some of the images from the mood boards to create posters in Photoshop, experimenting with different design elements to capture the experimental essence of Nasmak PM's music and visually represent their unique style.

Reflection

I found this project both exciting and challenging due to the unique nature of our client. While their distinctive style gave me plenty of room for creativity—something I don’t always experience with other clients—it was difficult to settle on a single concept. Nasmak’s ‘weird yet cool’ vibe required a media campaign that felt equally unique while remaining professional. Attending their rehearsals and speaking with the band members helped me connect with their vision, but I noticed some of my group members struggled to fully grasp their style, as it may not align with everyone’s tastes.

Despite the challenges, I am proud of the final outcome. I believe our campaign addressed the band’s needs while staying true to their unconventional aesthetic. Toon, Nasmak’s frontman, seemed very pleased and amused with our work, which was incredibly rewarding. Overall, I think our campaign stood out for its originality, reflecting the band’s personality and setting it apart from what the rest of the class produced.

PROJECT THREE/

Development

CLIENT/

Nasmak PM

PROJECT OVERVIEW

I developed a web app which allowed Nasmak PM to create custom, interactive videos to be used on their social media platforms.

SKILLS USED

  • JavaScript
  • HTML
  • CSS

DATE

13 November 2024 -
7 December 2024

How I started

My teammate and I worked together on the Media Campaign and so we had a clear idea as to what we wanted to do for the development project. We both wanted to create something that would be useful to the client and help them combat their challenge which was reaching more audiences.

So, we decided to create an app for them to create videos with the mascots which we thought of in the previous project for their social media platforms. With both of us having limited skills in JavaScript, we knew that creating this interactive app would be a challenge but we were determined to take on the challenge.

Prototyping

After splitting tasks, I worked on a total of three prototypes, starting from a basic proof of concept with just shapes into a fully functional final prototype that allowed users to move the sprite around and change size and character. I also made sure to conduct User Testing on these prototypes to ensure the program would be easy to operate and user friendly.

The final product

Using a combination of the code from our previous prototypes, feedback from the user testing and suggestions from teachers, we created our final version of the PMRecorder. This version included a control panel which allows users to easily change the size of the sprite and even the speed at which it moves. It also provides a button to toggle between different chacters and has a background color changer. This allows the user to create the video on a green screen and then fill it with any background they desire.

The control panel also has a record/stop/download button that makes the recording process easy and efficient to use. There are also insctructions to exmplain how to use the program aswell as which keys have functions.

"I am very pleased with this application as it is something that we can use right away. I am very excited to show the rest of the band members!"

- Toon, Nasmak PM

Version Control & GIT

My teammate and I utilized Git throughout our project to ensure seamless collaboration and version control. We began by creating a shared repository on GitLab. This allowed both of us to have access to the project's codebase. After setting up the repository, we cloned it locally using Git, enabling us to work on the code independently while keeping everything synchronized.

We made sure that each commit message was descriptive and followed a clear format, like 'add sprite toggling functionality' or 'resolve merge issue'. This made it easy to review the history of changes and understand what each update was about. Occasionally, we encountered merge conflicts when both of us edited the same part of the code but with the help of research and ChatGPT, we were always able to solve the issues. Using Git's version history, we could always revert to an earlier state of the project if something broke. This gave us confidence to experiment without the fear of losing stable code.

This was one of my first time collaborating with someone using git and so there were places where I want to improve on. For example, we worked on the same branch for everything but after research I learned that this is not good practice and that in the future, we should have separate branches for specific features or tasks.

Reflection

In the beginning of this project, it was quite challenging as my JavaScript skills were very entry level and this project was quite advanced for me. I really enjoyed challenging myself and figuring out how to do each step. I am very happy with my process and how I created prototypes in order to understand each concept.

In the end, I feel that this project stood out amongst the other projects in the class as it was completely different and unique. Due to this, the client was very enthusiast about our app as it was something the band could use immediately as a tool. The teachers were also very impressed and even invited my partner and I to showcase this project at the open day in 2025. This was very reassuring and rewarding for us, as it was a risky project that paid off in the end.

Dutch Design Week

One of my favourite aspects of Eindhoven is the vibrant and unique events hosted in the city, especially Dutch Design Week. Last year was my first time attending this incredible event, and I enjoyed it so much that I planned to buy a ticket this year. To my delight, I found out that Fontys was offering tickets to some students, and I jumped at the opportunity. I was so excited that I even persuaded a few friends to join me.

Dutch Design Week showcased a large variety of exhibits, and while everything was captivating, I focused on installations and products related to technology—areas closely tied to my studies. My journey began at Klokgebouw and around Strijp S, where several exhibitions immediately caught my attention.

Some of the highlights for me included a face-tracking project that mirrored users' emotions and several mechatronics projects featuring moving parts like robotic hands and heads as part of art installations. One exhibit in particular (pictured on the left) reminded me of "Can’t Help Myself" by Sun Yuan and Peng Yu—a famous artwork I studied in high school. This piece explores a machine's inability to deviate from its programmed task, and it was fascinating to see a similar concept come to life in a different medium. You can read more about the original artwork here .

I was also intrigued by the many innovative projects utilizing 3D printing, especially those made with biodegradable filaments and natural materials. While not directly tied to Media Design, this topic resonated with me because I’ve previously worked on projects, speeches, and essays about biofabrication. Seeing these concepts brought to life was exciting, and I enjoyed engaging with the designers to discuss their techniques and ideas.

Another exhibit that stood out featured a design concept for an app aimed at providing guidance, comfort, and relaxation for individuals experiencing anxiety in crowded public spaces (agoraphobia). Described as an “invisible friend,” the app offers motivation to leave safe spaces and venture out to overcome anxiety. Although I don’t personally face these challenges, I found the concept incredibly thoughtful and innovative. The app’s design was visually appealing, with charming little characters that added a comforting touch.

DESIGN PROCESS

After completing my research and establishing a clear direction, I moved into the design phase. I began by developing a stylescape to set the visual tone and guide the aesthetic approach, which then led into creating the logo. Throughout this process, I gathered feedback not only from the client but also from industry professionals and instructors, ensuring the design was refined and aligned with both the client’s vision and professional standards.

“The combination of colors is really effective. It does not deviate much from the Fontys house style, but it still gives a fresh feeling. The addition of photographs also gives this set-up a personal character. The schematic lamp with care cross in the middle is a nice variation on the lamp we have.”

-Client

What did i do with this feedback?

After analyzing both the client and survey feedback, I developed two new iterations of the stylescape to present to the client. These stylescapes featured a blend of the color palettes from the survey, chosen for their strong alignment with the client's core values and brand characteristics.

“I am a big fan of the blue colour palette. I feel that this gives the minor a fresh look without deviating too far away from the original Fontys colours.”

-Client

Logo Creation

I began the logo creation process by iterating on the client’s existing logo, incorporating a cross shape and circuit elements. However, I realised that this design was too similar to the original and according to the interviews which i conducted, the original design lacked originality. I then came up with the concept of representing innovation through a lightbulb, with the cross symbolising healthcare as the filament inside.

This idea creates a new logo appearence, while still containing some nods to the original design. After tracing a reference image of a lightbulb in Illustrator, I incorporated the cross shape as the filament. This concept was presented to the client, who provided positive feedback. I further refined the design by experimenting with gaps in the filament lines and variations in the lightbulb base. After gathering feedback from Josh and my peers, I finalised the logo.

Brand Guide

Once the logo, color palette, and style direction were confirmed, I collaborated with another group member to create the Brand Guide. We outlined essential brand elements like typography, logo usage, color variations, and visual themes, ensuring consistency and clarity across applications. This process improved my understanding of brand cohesion and gave me valuable teamwork experience in crafting a professional, cohesive guide.

Tested with Users and Stakeholders

Testing was a crucial part of my process across all projects to ensure that the final interactive products were user-friendly and aligned with their intended purposes. Some instances include in Project Three where I asked users to look at the posters I had made and tell me what aspects they felt were lacking in order to get maximum interaction with my final iterations. I also conducted a full User Test on my final prototype for my Recorder App in Project Three where I created a list of instructions for a user (who fit into the target audience) to follow and watched how they interacted with the program. Similarly I conducted another user test for my portfolio navigation, in order to see if users could easily navigate from one project to the next as my design is quite minimal. Like the previous test, I formulated a list of instructions for the user to follow and then watched their interactions. This helped me make small design tweaks to ensure my portfolio was user friendly.

Exploratory Research

Throughout my projects, I conducted exploratory research to gain a deeper understanding of the topics and challenges involved. In Project One, I gathered insights from the target audience through interviews to understand their preferences and thought processes when selecting a minor study. This approach aligned with the Field Research Method from the DOT framework. The information collected was critically evaluated by cross-referencing it with reliable industry sources, ensuring that my design decisions were well-grounded.

In Project Two, exploratory research focused on understanding the band’s audience and demographic. This involved analyzing existing online data about the preferences of the target audience, studying promotional strategies, and identifying new opportunities, such as collaborating with local social media pages like @eindhovencity. This process exemplifies the Library Method from the DOT framework.

For Project Three, I created multiple prototype iterations, which served as a form of research by allowing me to test and refine various approaches—an application of the Workshop Method. These iterations provided valuable insights on how to achieve the desired final outcome. Additionally, the usability tests I conducted during both Project Three and my portfolio development demonstrate exploratory research through the Lab Method, as they provided crucial feedback for improving functionality and user experience.

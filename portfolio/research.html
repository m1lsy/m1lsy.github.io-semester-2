Reasearch Process

To begin the rebranding process, I started by making use of the Field Research Method and interviewing people who fit into the target audience. This consisted of students from various studies who had recently completed or were in the process of choosing their minor study.

KEY POINTS DISCUSSED IN INTERVIEWS

1. Search Process & Selection Factors:

The students used the website Keesopminor.nl to explore various minors within the Netherlands. A primary factor for one of the students was finding a minor that emphasized creativity. They also utilized social media platforms like Instagram and YouTube to gain insights into the student experience for each minor, particularly valuing "day in the life" content that showcased student projects and activities

2. First Impressions of the Minor:

The Health Innovation Minor, which combines technology and healthcare, appeared highly innovative and unique, allowing for collaboration with students from different fields. However, it was noted that this minor would primarily appeal to those interested in the healthcare industry, which didn’t align with the specific interests of one of the students.

3. Description of the Minor for Peers:

The minor was described as a healthcare-focused program with an emphasis on technology, design, and business development, aimed at generating new ideas to tackle medical challenges.

4. Clarity of the Minor's Webpage:

Both students felt that the website provided a thorough explanation of the minor’s scope and objectives, albeit with extensive text. They suggested that additional visuals would help clarify what the program entails, especially for creative-minded students.

5. Branding Feedback:

The students had mixed opinions about the minor's branding. While one found the logo to effectively represent healthcare and innovation (using a cross symbol and circuit lines), another felt the logo and colors were too generic and could benefit from a more unique approach.

These interviews were valuable for my rebranding project as they provided direct insights into how students perceive the Health Innovation Minor and its current branding. The feedback on the website’s clarity and the desire for more visuals highlighted the need for a design that communicates the program’s innovative nature more effectively, especially for creative-minded students.

Additionally, the mixed opinions on the logo and branding pointed to an opportunity for a fresher, more unique approach to better resonate with the target audience. Understanding students' decision-making process, including their preference for content on social media, helped guide the direction for a rebrand that would be more engaging and aligned with their interests.

The Next Step

After conducting the interviews and analysing the results, I started defining the core values of the brand in order to help focus the design direction. Once the core values were defined, we made a few colour palette options which we felt reflected these values. We created a survey and people who fit within the demographic of our target audience to select which adjectives they felt best described the colours.

SURVEY RESULTS

Based on the survey results, it became clear that color palettes featuring shades of blues and greens most closely aligned with the values of the program. As a result, we chose to focus on these colors in our design concepts, ensuring that the final look would resonate with the program's identity and appeal to its target audience.
